The Wicked Problem

A wicked problem is an issue that is perhaps borderline impossible to solve because of incomplete, contradictory, and changing requirements that are often difficult to recognize

The wicked problem I chose to consult is the acceptance of bribes in authoritative positions. This refers to anyone with a bit of power and/or 'prestige' over the common man, such as police officers, government officials, etcetera. Corruption becomes a prominent result of accepted bribes amongst these positions of power, so often the two are spoken of in tandem as the former seldom exists without the latter (one may even define the act of bribing as the act of corrupting). In 2019, within the United States, there were 252 recorded bribery offenders sentenced under US Sentence Commissioning Guidelines.
