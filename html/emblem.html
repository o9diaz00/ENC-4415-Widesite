Emblem

Across my four quadrants of the pop-cycle, a repeating signifier unveiled was heroism. Justice, heroism, and words of that nature are not necesarily defined words, in fact, one would mostly have to give an example of these words in order to accurately define them (they are signified rather than signifiers)...

Having said that, I took my analysis and decided upon an image that could represent such notions and at the same time befit me and my train of thoughts --- such was a cape. A superhero is, conceptually, the epitome of heroism, and the cape is a distinguishing feature on many popular superheroes.

Unfortunately, as far as we know, superheroes only exist within our fictional media. The power to create such an entity lies in one's imagination, and what better tool to bring such to life with the grandest of creativity and vigor, than art? Hence, the paint brush debuting here, but this is not the sole reasoning.

Interpreting Meaning

As stated prior, a superhero is the epitome of heroism, however, they only exist in our fictitious media, and reality's heroes are the ones whom are succumbing and participating in the wicked problem. A paint brush emerging as my emblem unifies the existence of a superhero with my relationship with art. Essentially a superhero(es) is designed to solve any and all problems, with my art, I can create such and in turn, a solution for every problem. This however will only exist outside the realms of reality, which drives home the second portion of this emblem's meaning. Some problems just cannot be solved as the desire to keep them, or the privileges that contributes or creates the problematic phenomenon itself, outweighs the desire to rectify said issue. The only real solutions exist outside the realms of reality; coming to terms with such lets me know that I must cope and coexist with the problem, and should I want to find a resolution, I have to look where the imagination runs rampart and are not bound by any limitations --- this outlet would be my art.

In a nutshell, how does the paint brush help me combat bribery? It does not, at least not outwardly. However, it allows me to create a veil that assists me with maintaining stability whilst this emergent problem exists. My being, remaining healthy and stable is the first and perhaps most important step and attribute that allows me to even try to plot against this villain
